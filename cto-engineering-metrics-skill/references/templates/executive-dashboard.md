## Using This Dashboard

### Monthly Review Process

**Week 1**: Collect data

- Pull metrics from systems
- Survey team for satisfaction
- Review incidents and post-mortems
- Compile feature delivery list

**Week 2**: Analysis

- Calculate trends
- Identify wins and concerns
- Prepare recommendations
- Review with engineering leadership

**Week 3**: Present to executives

- 15-minute presentation
- 15-minute Q&A
- Share dashboard ahead of meeting

**Week 4**: Follow-up

- Share detailed appendix if requested
- Address action items from discussion

---

### Customization Guide

**For Early-Stage Startups**:

- Focus on: Feature velocity, quality, team satisfaction
- De-emphasize: Cost metrics, DORA metrics
- Keep it to 1 page

**For Growth-Stage Companies**:

- Use this template as-is
- Add custom business metrics if applicable

**For Enterprise**:

- Add team-by-team breakdown
- Include compliance and security sections
- Expand to 3-4 pages

---

### Red Flags to Watch

| Red Flag                      | Likely Issue               | Immediate Action                           |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Satisfaction <7 and declining | Burnout, unclear direction | 1-on-1s with team, address top pain points |
| Uptime <99.5% for 2 months    | Infrastructure issues      | Incident review, hire SRE                  |
| Cost per customer rising      | Inefficient scaling        | Architecture review, optimization sprint   |
| Bugs increasing for 3 months  | Quality issues             | Review testing process, add coverage       |
| Features delivered declining  | Blockers or complexity     | Identify bottlenecks, simplify             |
| Attrition >15% annually       | Culture or comp issues     | Exit interviews, market comp review        |

---

### Communication Tips

**For CEO**:

- Lead with business impact
- Connect engineering work to revenue/growth
- Highlight risks and mitigation plans
- Be specific about resource needs

**For CFO**:

- Emphasize cost efficiency
- Show ROI of engineering investments
- Explain budget variances
- Demonstrate fiscal responsibility

**For Board** (Quarterly):

- Strategic initiatives progress
- Key hires and team growth
- Major technical decisions
- Competitive positioning
