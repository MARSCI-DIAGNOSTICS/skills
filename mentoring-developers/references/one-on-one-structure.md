# 1:1 Meeting Structure

Templates and guidance for running effective 1:1 meetings in mentoring relationships.

## Core Principles

### It's Their Meeting

The 1:1 belongs to the mentee, not the mentor.

**Mentee drives the agenda.** They decide what's most important to discuss.

**Mentor facilitates.** You ask questions, provide perspective, remove blockers.

**Exception:** If there's something critical you need to address (performance, urgent feedback), you can add it - but their topics come first.

### Consistency Matters

1:1s should be:

- **Regular:** Weekly is ideal for active mentoring relationships
- **Protected:** Don't cancel frequently; reschedule if needed
- **Predictable:** Same time, same length, same structure

Missing or canceling 1:1s sends a message that the relationship isn't a priority.

### Not for Status Updates

Use async updates (Slack, email) for status. 1:1s are for:

- Career development
- Blockers and challenges
- Feedback (both directions)
- Relationship building
- Strategic thinking

## Meeting Templates

### Standard 30-Minute 1:1

```markdown
## 1:1 Agenda - [Date]

### Check-in (5 min)
- How are you doing? (General wellbeing)
- Any wins from this week you want to share?

### Their Topics (10-15 min)
[Mentee adds items here before the meeting]
-
-
-

### Development Focus (10 min)
- Progress on current goals
- Challenges or blockers
- Skills they want to develop

### Action Items
- [ ] [Mentor]
- [ ] [Mentee]
```

### Kickoff 1:1 (First Meeting)

For establishing a new mentoring relationship:

```markdown
## Mentoring Kickoff - [Date]

### Getting to Know Each Other (15 min)
- Background: What brought you here?
- Interests: What aspects of tech excite you?
- Goals: Where do you want to be in 1-2 years?

### Expectations (10 min)
- What do you hope to get from this mentoring relationship?
- What's worked/not worked in past mentoring?
- How do you prefer to receive feedback?

### Logistics (5 min)
- Meeting frequency and duration
- Communication preferences (Slack? Email?)
- How to cancel/reschedule

### Immediate Priorities (10 min)
- What's the most pressing thing you're working on?
- Where are you feeling stuck right now?
- What would you like help with first?

### Action Items
- [ ] [Mentor] Set up recurring 1:1s
- [ ] [Mentee] Prepare topics for next 1:1
- [ ] Both: Read/share any relevant documents
```

### Growth-Focused 1:1

For occasional deep-dives on development:

```markdown
## Growth 1:1 - [Date]

### Reflection (10 min)
- What's been going well lately?
- What's been challenging?
- What have you learned recently?

### Skills Assessment (15 min)
- What technical skills do you want to develop?
- What soft skills need attention?
- What's blocking your growth?

### Goal Setting (15 min)
- 3-month goals: What do you want to accomplish?
- Stretch assignments: What would challenge you?
- Support needed: How can I help?

### Action Items
- [ ] [Mentee] Draft SMART goals
- [ ] [Mentor] Identify stretch opportunities
- [ ] Both: Review in 4 weeks
```

### Check-In After Difficult Event

For processing after setbacks, production incidents, or tough feedback:

```markdown
## Check-In - [Date]

### How Are You Doing? (10 min)
- Open-ended: let them process
- Normalize feelings: "That's a hard thing to go through"
- Don't rush to solutions

### What Happened (10 min)
- Their perspective on the situation
- What they wish had gone differently
- What was out of their control

### Lessons & Next Steps (10 min)
- What did they learn?
- What would they do differently?
- What support do they need?

### Action Items
- [ ] [Any follow-ups]
```

## SMART Goals for Mentees

Help mentees set goals that are:

| Letter | Meaning | Example |
| ------ | ------- | ------- |
| **S**pecific | Clearly defined | "Lead a code review for a PR with 500+ lines" |
| **M**easurable | Can track progress | "Complete 3 tech talks by Q2" |
| **A**chievable | Realistic | "Get promoted to Senior" (in 18 months, not 2) |
| **R**elevant | Aligned with their path | Connected to career goals |
| **T**ime-bound | Has a deadline | "By end of Q1" |

**Poor goal:** "Get better at system design"
**Better goal:** "Lead the design doc for one project by end of Q2, getting feedback from at least 2 senior engineers"

## Questions to Ask

### Understanding Their World

- What's taking most of your energy right now?
- What are you excited about?
- What are you worried about?
- If you could change one thing about your work, what would it be?

### Identifying Growth Areas

- What skills do you wish you were better at?
- What do you avoid that you know you should do?
- What feedback have you gotten recently?
- Who do you admire and what do they do well?

### Providing Support

- How can I be more helpful to you?
- What's blocking you that I could help remove?
- What do you need from me that you're not getting?
- Is there anything I do that's unhelpful?

### Checking the Relationship

- Are these 1:1s useful? How could they be better?
- Is there anything you've wanted to bring up but haven't?
- Do you feel comfortable giving me feedback?

## Note-Taking

Keep shared notes for each 1:1:

**Benefits:**

- Track progress over time
- Remember commitments
- Identify patterns
- Prepare for performance reviews

**Structure:**

```markdown
## 1:1 Notes - [Mentee Name]

### [Date]
**Topics discussed:**
- [Topic 1]
- [Topic 2]

**Key takeaways:**
- [Insight]

**Action items:**
- [ ] [Owner] Task
- [ ] [Owner] Task

---

### [Previous Date]
...
```

**Where to keep them:**

- Shared doc (Google Doc, Notion) - both can access and add
- Make clear these are between the two of you, not shared widely

## When Engagement Wanes

Signs the relationship is struggling:

- Mentee stops bringing topics
- Conversations feel routine
- Meetings frequently rescheduled
- Little progress on goals

**Check-in script:**

> "I've noticed our 1:1s have felt a bit routine lately. I want to make sure this time is valuable for you. What would make these more useful? Is there something you'd like to focus on that we haven't been?"

**Options:**

- Change the format (more/less structured)
- Change the frequency
- Focus on a specific project together
- Acknowledge it may have run its course

## Celebrating Achievements

Don't let wins pass unremarked:

- Acknowledge completed goals
- Celebrate growth you've observed
- Note milestones (first production deploy, first tech talk, etc.)
- Share their wins with leadership (with permission)

**Script:**
> "Before we dive in, I wanted to acknowledge how much progress you've made on [X]. Three months ago you were struggling with [Y], and now you're doing [Z] confidently. That's real growth."
