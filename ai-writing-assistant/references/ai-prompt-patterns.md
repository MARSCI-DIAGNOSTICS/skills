# AI Prompt Patterns for Professional Writing

Comprehensive prompt templates organized by use case for AI-assisted professional writing.

## How to Use These Patterns

1. **Select the pattern** that matches your task
2. **Customize placeholders** with your specific context
3. **Add constraints** that matter for your situation
4. **Iterate** on the output with follow-up prompts
5. **Verify and personalize** the result

## Email Prompt Patterns

### Pattern: Status Update Email

```text
Write a project status update email to [stakeholder role].

Project: [Project name]
Current status: [On track / At risk / Delayed]
Key updates:
- [Update 1]
- [Update 2]
- [Update 3]

Blockers (if any): [List or "None"]
Next steps: [What's happening next]
Ask (if any): [What you need from them]

Tone: Professional, confident
Length: 150-200 words maximum
```

### Pattern: Request Email

```text
Write an email requesting [what you need] from [recipient role].

Context: [Why you need this]
Deadline: [When you need it]
Why it matters: [Impact/importance]
What you're offering: [How you'll reciprocate/what you've done]

Make the ask clear in the first paragraph.
Keep it under 150 words.
End with specific next step.
```

### Pattern: Follow-Up Email

```text
Write a follow-up email for [previous communication].

Original topic: [What you discussed/requested]
Time since last contact: [X days/weeks]
Current status: [What you know]
What you need: [Response, decision, action]

Tone: Polite but direct
Don't be apologetic, be professional.
Include easy way for them to respond.
Keep under 100 words.
```

### Pattern: Rejection/Decline Email

```text
Write an email declining [request/invitation].

What I'm declining: [Specific request]
Why (if appropriate to share): [Brief reason]
Alternative I can offer (if any): [What you CAN do]

Requirements:
- Be gracious, not apologetic
- Be clear (don't leave room for misunderstanding)
- Keep the relationship positive
- Keep under 100 words
```

### Pattern: Introduction/Connection Email

```text
Write an email introducing myself to [person/role].

Who I am: [Your role, relevant background]
Why I'm reaching out: [Specific reason]
What I'm hoping for: [Clear ask - meeting, chat, advice]
Connection point (if any): [Mutual contact, shared interest, their work]

Make it personal, not template-feeling.
Show I've done research on them.
Keep under 150 words.
Clear call to action at end.
```

## Feedback Prompt Patterns

### Pattern: Performance Feedback

```text
Help me write constructive feedback for [team member role].

Context: [Setting - 1:1, performance review, etc.]

Positive observations:
- [Specific example of good work]
- [Another example]

Areas for growth:
- [Specific behavior to address]
- [Impact of that behavior]
- [Desired change]

Use the SBI model (Situation-Behavior-Impact).
Be specific with examples.
Focus on behaviors, not personality.
End with clear, actionable suggestion.
```

### Pattern: Peer Feedback

```text
Write feedback for a colleague's [work product - code review, document, presentation].

What I'm reviewing: [Brief description]

What works well:
- [Specific positive]
- [Another positive]

Suggestions for improvement:
- [Specific suggestion with reasoning]
- [Another suggestion]

Tone: Collaborative, not critical
Frame suggestions as questions where appropriate.
Lead with positives.
Be specific about what and why.
```

### Pattern: Upward Feedback

```text
Help me structure feedback for my manager about [topic].

What I want to address:
- [Specific issue or observation]

Impact on me/team:
- [How this affects work]

What I'd like to see:
- [Specific request or suggestion]

Tone: Respectful, constructive
Frame as observations, not accusations.
Focus on impact and solutions.
Keep it professional.
```

## Presentation Prompt Patterns

### Pattern: Full Outline

```text
Create an outline for a [X]-minute presentation on [topic].

Audience: [Who they are]
Their knowledge level: [Beginner/Intermediate/Expert]
What they care about: [Their priorities/pain points]
Goal: [What should they know/do after]

Structure:
1. WHAT (10%): Hook and problem statement
2. WHY (30%): Context and stakes
3. HOW (50%): Solution and details
4. CLOSE (10%): Takeaways and call to action

Include timing for each section.
Suggest transitions between sections.
Include 3 specific takeaways.
```

### Pattern: Hook Generator

```text
Generate 5 different hooks for a presentation about [topic].

Audience: [Who they are]
The main problem/opportunity: [Core message]

Types to include:
1. Story-based hook (brief anecdote)
2. Surprising statistic (make up placeholder, I'll verify)
3. Provocative question
4. Common misconception to challenge
5. "Imagine if..." scenario

Each should be 2-3 sentences maximum.
Must grab attention in first 10 seconds.
```

### Pattern: Section Expansion

```text
Expand this presentation section into speaker notes and slide content.

Section title: [Title]
Key point: [Main message for this section]
Evidence/examples to include: [What you want covered]
Time allocation: [X minutes]

Provide:
1. 3-4 slide titles (one idea each)
2. Bullet points for each slide (5-7 words per bullet)
3. Speaker notes (what to say, not read)
4. Transition to next section
```

## Content Prompt Patterns

### Pattern: LinkedIn Post (Experience)

```text
Create a LinkedIn post about [experience/lesson].

What happened: [Brief story]
The lesson: [Key insight]
Why it matters: [For the audience]
My unique angle: [What makes my perspective different]

Structure:
- Hook (2 lines that make them click "see more")
- Story (brief context)
- Insight (the learning)
- CTA (question to drive comments)

Length: 150-250 words
Add line breaks for readability.
End with 3-5 relevant hashtags.
```

### Pattern: LinkedIn Post (Opinion)

```text
Create a LinkedIn post sharing my opinion on [topic].

My take: [Your actual position]
Why I think this: [Your reasoning]
Counterargument I acknowledge: [The other side]
What I've learned/observed: [Evidence]

Structure:
- Contrarian opening (grab attention)
- My position (clear statement)
- Supporting evidence (2-3 points)
- Nuanced conclusion
- Question for discussion

Keep it professional, not inflammatory.
Acknowledge complexity.
Invite genuine discussion.
```

### Pattern: Technical Documentation

```text
Write documentation for [feature/API/system].

Purpose: [What it does]
Audience: [Who will read this]
Prerequisite knowledge: [What they should already know]

Include:
1. Overview (2-3 sentences)
2. Prerequisites (what they need)
3. Quick start (fastest path to working)
4. Detailed explanation (how it works)
5. Common use cases (examples)
6. Troubleshooting (common issues)

Use clear headings.
Include code examples.
Keep language simple and direct.
```

## Refinement Prompt Patterns

### Pattern: Clarity Improvement

```text
Improve this text for clarity:

[Paste your draft]

Requirements:
- Remove jargon (or explain it)
- Shorten sentences
- Use active voice
- Make the main point obvious
- Keep under [X] words

Preserve my tone and key messages.
Highlight changes you made.
```

### Pattern: Tone Adjustment

```text
Adjust the tone of this text to be more [target tone]:

[Paste your draft]

Target tone: [professional/casual/urgent/empathetic/direct]
Current issue: [What's wrong with current tone]
Keep: [Elements to preserve]

Show before/after for key phrases.
Explain why changes improve tone.
```

### Pattern: Condensing

```text
Condense this text to [target length/percentage]:

[Paste your draft]

Priority information (must keep):
- [Key point 1]
- [Key point 2]

Nice-to-have (cut if needed):
- [Less important point]

Keep the core message intact.
Preserve my voice.
Maintain clarity (don't sacrifice understanding for brevity).
```

### Pattern: Expansion

```text
Expand this text with more detail:

[Paste your brief version]

Add:
- Examples for: [Which points need examples]
- Explanation of: [What needs more context]
- Transitions between: [Sections that feel abrupt]

Target length: [X words/paragraphs]
Keep the same structure and voice.
Don't add filler - only substantive content.
```

## Multi-Turn Prompt Strategies

### Strategy: Iterative Refinement

```text
Turn 1: Generate initial draft
Turn 2: "Make the opening more engaging"
Turn 3: "Shorten the middle section by 30%"
Turn 4: "Add a specific example in paragraph 2"
Turn 5: "Check tone - is this too formal?"
```

### Strategy: Version Comparison

```text
Turn 1: Generate version A (formal)
Turn 2: "Now write version B - same content, more casual"
Turn 3: "Compare versions A and B - which is better for [context]?"
Turn 4: "Combine the best elements of both"
```

### Strategy: Role-Based Review

```text
Turn 1: Generate the content
Turn 2: "Read this as [skeptical executive] - what questions would they have?"
Turn 3: "Now read as [busy engineer] - what would confuse them?"
Turn 4: "Address the concerns from both perspectives"
```

## Meta-Prompts

### Get AI to Write Prompts

```text
I need to [task description].

What information would you need from me to write an effective [email/post/document] for this?

Ask me the questions, then I'll provide answers, and you can write the content.
```

### Improve Your Prompts

```text
I've been using this prompt:

[Your current prompt]

It's not working well because [issue].
How can I improve it to get better results?
```

### Create Reusable Template

```text
I frequently need to write [type of content].

Create a reusable prompt template I can fill in each time.
Include:
- Required information
- Optional customizations
- Output format specifications
- Quality checks to include
```
