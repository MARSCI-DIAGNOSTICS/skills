# Voice Preservation Techniques

How to maintain your authentic voice while using AI writing assistance.

## Why Voice Matters

**Voice is your brand.**

When you use AI tools without voice preservation:

- Emails sound generic and impersonal
- LinkedIn posts blend into the noise
- Colleagues notice something "off"
- Trust erodes ("Did they actually write this?")
- Your unique perspective gets lost

When you preserve your voice:

- Content feels authentically you
- Relationships stay personal
- Your perspective comes through
- People remember your communication
- AI becomes invisible assistance

## The Voice Analysis Process

### Step 1: Collect Voice Samples

Gather 5-10 examples of your best writing:

- Emails you're proud of
- Slack messages that got great responses
- Documents you wrote from scratch
- LinkedIn posts that performed well
- Presentations you developed

**Selection criteria:**

- Written entirely by you (no AI assistance)
- Representative of your natural style
- From different contexts (to show range)
- Recent (your voice evolves)

### Step 2: Analyze Your Patterns

**Use this prompt to analyze your samples:**

```text
Analyze my writing style from these samples. For each category,
identify specific patterns with examples:

[Paste 3-5 samples]

Analyze:
1. SENTENCE STRUCTURE
   - Average sentence length
   - Variation patterns
   - Use of fragments
   - Complex vs simple sentences

2. WORD CHOICE
   - Formality level
   - Jargon usage
   - Preferred verbs (active/passive)
   - Distinctive phrases I repeat

3. OPENINGS AND CLOSINGS
   - How I typically start
   - How I typically end
   - Transition patterns

4. PERSONALITY MARKERS
   - Use of humor
   - Directness level
   - Personal disclosure style
   - Enthusiasm expression

5. STRUCTURAL PREFERENCES
   - Paragraph length
   - Use of lists
   - Heading style
   - Formatting habits

Provide a "voice profile" I can use in future prompts.
```

### Step 3: Create Your Voice Profile

Based on analysis, create a reusable voice profile:

**Example Voice Profile:**

```text
MY VOICE PROFILE:

Tone: Direct and conversational, professional but not stiff
Sentence length: Mix of short punchy and medium; rarely long complex
Word choice: Plain language, occasional technical terms, no corporate jargon
Personality: Dry humor, occasional self-deprecation, enthusiasm for solutions
Openings: Get to the point quickly, no lengthy preambles
Closings: Clear next steps, often ends with a question
Patterns:
- Use "Here's the thing:" before key points
- Favor bullet points over long paragraphs
- Use contractions (don't, won't, can't)
- Occasional em-dashes for emphasis
- Tend to acknowledge tradeoffs/nuance

AVOID (not my voice):
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "Per our discussion"
- "Going forward"
- Excessive exclamation points
- Corporate buzzwords (leverage, synergy, etc.)
```

## Voice Injection Methods

### Method 1: Pre-Prompt Voice Profile

Include your voice profile at the start of every content request:

```text
Write in a style that matches this voice profile:

[Your voice profile]

Now write: [Your actual request]
```

### Method 2: Post-Draft Voice Edit

Have AI draft, then add your voice:

```text
Step 1: Get AI draft
Step 2: Read for generic phrases
Step 3: Replace with your actual expressions:
  - "I wanted to reach out" → "Quick question for you"
  - "Please let me know" → "What do you think?"
  - "I hope this helps" → "Let me know if this lands"
Step 4: Add personal details only you know
Step 5: Read aloud - does it sound like you?
```

### Method 3: Hybrid Paragraphs

Write key paragraphs yourself; let AI fill gaps:

```text
Complete this draft, matching my style in the paragraphs I wrote:

[PARAGRAPH 1 - I wrote this, keep exactly:]
Hey Sarah - wanted to loop back on the database thing
from Tuesday. Here's where we landed.

[PARAGRAPH 2 - AI draft this section about:]
Technical summary of the decision (3-4 sentences)

[PARAGRAPH 3 - I wrote this, keep exactly:]
I know this is a bigger change than we planned, but I think
it's the right call. Happy to walk through the details if helpful.

Match my tone and style in the AI sections.
```

### Method 4: Example-Driven Generation

Provide examples of how you'd write similar content:

```text
Write an email declining a meeting request.

Here's how I typically write decline emails:

EXAMPLE 1:
"Hey - I need to bow out of Thursday's sync. Slammed with
the release this week. Can we push to next Tuesday instead?"

EXAMPLE 2:
"Thanks for the invite, but I'm not the right person for this one.
Try Sarah - she's closer to the data pipeline stuff."

Now write a decline email for: [Your specific situation]
Match the style and tone of my examples.
```

### Method 5: Voice-Check Review

After AI drafts, run a voice-check:

```text
Compare this draft to my voice profile:

[Paste AI draft]

My voice profile:
[Paste profile]

Identify:
1. Phrases that don't match my voice
2. Sections that feel too formal/casual
3. Missing personality markers
4. Suggested replacements to match my style
```

## Specific Voice Elements

### Opening Lines

**Track how you actually start emails/messages:**

- "Quick question -"
- "Heads up on..."
- "Following up on..."
- "Hey - wanted to share..."

**Create a list of YOUR openers to use instead of AI defaults.**

### Transition Phrases

**Note your transition patterns:**

- "Here's the thing:"
- "That said,"
- "On the flip side,"
- "The challenge is..."

**Replace AI transitions with your actual phrases.**

### Closing Lines

**Document how you actually end things:**

- "Let me know what you think."
- "Thoughts?"
- "Happy to dig into this more if helpful."
- "Ping me if questions."

**Never use AI closings verbatim.**

### Personality Markers

**Identify what makes your writing YOU:**

- Specific metaphors you use
- Running jokes or references
- Level of formality/informality
- How you express uncertainty
- How you show enthusiasm

**Consciously add these after AI drafts.**

## Context-Specific Voice

### Email Voice

Your email voice might be:

- More casual with close colleagues
- More formal with executives
- Technical with engineers
- Plain language with non-technical stakeholders

**Create voice variants:**

```text
EMAIL VOICE - Close Team:
- First name only
- Casual tone, occasional emoji
- Skip pleasantries, get to point
- Can be blunt

EMAIL VOICE - Executives:
- Full greeting
- Professional but not stiff
- Bottom-line up front
- Specific asks/recommendations

EMAIL VOICE - External Partners:
- Proper greeting
- Slightly more formal
- Explicit context (they lack shared history)
- Clear next steps
```

### Presentation Voice

Your spoken/presentation voice might differ:

- More conversational
- Shorter sentences
- More rhetorical questions
- Stories and analogies

**Adjust voice profile for presentation content.**

### LinkedIn Voice

Your public professional voice might be:

- More polished than casual Slack
- More personality than formal docs
- Optimized for engagement
- Strategic in what you share

**Separate profile for social content.**

## Red Flags: AI Voice Leaking Through

### Generic Phrases to Eliminate

| AI Default | Replace With (your version) |
| ---------- | --------------------------- |
| "I hope this email finds you well" | [Delete or use your opener] |
| "I wanted to reach out" | [Get to the point] |
| "Please don't hesitate to" | "Let me know if" |
| "I would be happy to" | "Happy to" or "Can do" |
| "Going forward" | "From now on" or [delete] |
| "In order to" | "To" |
| "It's worth noting that" | [Just state it] |
| "I completely understand" | [Specific acknowledgment] |

### Over-Structured Content

**AI tendency:** Numbered lists for everything

**Your fix:** Use your natural formatting - maybe you prefer bullets, maybe prose, maybe a mix

### Excessive Hedging

**AI tendency:** "It might be worth considering perhaps..."

**Your fix:** Replace with your actual confidence level

### Missing Specifics

**AI tendency:** Generic examples and placeholders

**Your fix:** Add real names, dates, projects, details

## Workflow Integration

### Quick Voice Check (30 Seconds)

After any AI draft:

1. Read first sentence - does it sound like you?
2. Scan for generic phrases - mark for replacement
3. Check the close - is it your style?
4. Add one personal detail AI couldn't know

### Full Voice Edit (5 Minutes)

For important communications:

1. Read entire draft aloud
2. Mark phrases that feel "off"
3. Replace with your actual expressions
4. Add context and specifics
5. Inject personality markers
6. Final read - "Would I actually say this?"

### Voice Maintenance (Monthly)

Periodically refresh your voice:

1. Review recent writing samples
2. Update voice profile with new patterns
3. Note new phrases or expressions
4. Adjust for role/context changes

## When to Skip AI Entirely

Some content should be 100% human:

- **Highly personal feedback** - Your words matter
- **Relationship-critical messages** - Authenticity essential
- **Apologies** - Must be genuine
- **Creative content** - Your unique perspective is the point
- **Controversial topics** - Need your nuanced judgment
- **First impressions** - Set the tone with your voice

For these, write from scratch, perhaps using AI only for proofreading.

## The Authenticity Test

Before sending anything AI-assisted, ask:

1. Would my colleagues recognize this as my writing?
2. Would I be comfortable saying this in person?
3. Does anything feel like someone else wrote it?
4. Have I added something only I could add?
5. Am I proud to put my name on this?

**If any answer is "no," keep editing.**
